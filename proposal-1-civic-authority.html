The Issue

New York Moves — But Not Equally

01

When you walk through New York City, you feel possibility everywhere—but you also see the gaps. NYC's movement system is fragmented. Agencies, schools, facilities, community groups, and nonprofits all work hard, but they operate separately. The issue isn't talent or effort. It's misalignment.

School PE and student movement are shrinking every year

Physical education time has steadily decreased across many NYC schools, leaving students with fewer opportunities to move, play, and build lifelong healthy habits. The inequity is most visible in low-income districts where staffing, equipment, and facility access are limited.

Youth sports are increasingly privatized and out of reach

As community leagues shrink and private clubs expand, thousands of kids, especially in underserved neighborhoods, are priced out of structured sport. Access now depends more on income than interest or talent, leaving many youth without safe, consistent ways to become and stay active.

Seniors are trying to stay mobile with very little help

New York's older adults face barriers ranging from unsafe sidewalks to limited strength and balance programming. Many want to stay active but lack the structured support needed to maintain mobility, independence, and social connection.

Fitness options for New Yorkers with disabilities are extremely limited

Adaptive programs, accessible facilities, and trained staff remain unevenly distributed across the city. Too many New Yorkers with disabilities encounter physical, logistical, and cultural barriers that prevent full participation in movement, recreation, and sport.

Movement as a Right, Not a Privilege

My mission is to help build a coordinated movement ecosystem where every New Yorker, in every borough, at every age, of every ability has access to safe, meaningful, culturally relevant opportunities to move.

The Solution

A Citywide Movement Framework

Beyond the Silos

Sports and recreation are often viewed as "nice to have," but they are among NYC's strongest tools for improving public health, mental wellbeing, school outcomes, workforce performance, and community safety. My vision unifies these pieces into a coordinated, citywide movement ecosystem that serves every New Yorker with clarity, consistency, and access.

The Moment

Why This Moment Matters

New York City is at an inflection point.

Rising inactivity, mental health strain, and widening inequity threaten the wellbeing of millions. At the same time, we're seeing unprecedented innovation, community energy, and public interest in wellness.

The opportunity is clear. We can connect schools, parks, community centers, public facilities, healthcare, and workplaces into a coordinated movement ecosystem that serves every neighborhood and every New Yorker.
