NEW YORK MOVES — BUT NOT EQUALLY

NYC's movement system is fragmented. Agencies, schools, facilities, community groups, and nonprofits all work hard, but they operate separately. The issue isn't talent or effort. It's misalignment.

01

SCHOOL PE IS SHRINKING

Physical education time has steadily decreased across many NYC schools, leaving students with fewer opportunities to move, play, and build lifelong healthy habits. The inequity is most visible in low-income districts.

02

YOUTH SPORTS PRIVATIZED

As community leagues shrink and private clubs expand, thousands of kids—especially in underserved neighborhoods—are priced out of structured sport. Access depends more on income than interest or talent.

03

SENIORS LACK SUPPORT

New York's older adults face barriers from unsafe sidewalks to limited programming. Many want to stay active but lack the structured support needed to maintain mobility and independence.

04

DISABILITY ACCESS LIMITED

Adaptive programs and accessible facilities remain unevenly distributed. Too many New Yorkers with disabilities encounter barriers that prevent full participation in movement and recreation.

MOVEMENT AS A RIGHT, NOT A PRIVILEGE

My mission is to help build a coordinated movement ecosystem where every New Yorker, in every borough, at every age, of every ability has access to safe, meaningful, culturally relevant opportunities to move.

The Solution

A CITYWIDE MOVEMENT FRAMEWORK

Sports and recreation are often viewed as "nice to have," but they are among NYC's strongest tools for improving public health, mental wellbeing, school outcomes, workforce performance, and community safety.

01

ACCESS & EQUITY

Ensuring every New Yorker—across every borough, age, income level, and ability—has real opportunities to move.

02

COMMUNITY INFRASTRUCTURE

Modernizing and activating the parks, facilities, and public spaces that shape our daily lives.

03

LIFELONG PERFORMANCE

Movement pathways through every stage of life—from youth play to adult recreation to aging well.

The Moment

WHY THIS MOMENT MATTERS

New York City is at an inflection point.

Rising inactivity, mental health strain, and widening inequity threaten the wellbeing of millions. At the same time, we're seeing unprecedented innovation, community energy, and public interest in wellness.

The opportunity is clear. We can connect schools, parks, community centers, public facilities, healthcare, and workplaces into a coordinated movement ecosystem that serves every neighborhood and every New Yorker.
